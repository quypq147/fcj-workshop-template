## 2. Main Content of the Event

The event featured deep-dive presentations and panel discussions from leading industry experts on Cloud, Data, AI, and career development:

1. **Cloud Industry Overview & Hiring Trends** - Speaker: **Nguyễn Gia Hưng (Hưng Gia)** - Head of Solution Architect at AWS Vietnam
   
   - **Market Shift:** The transition to "Cloud First" is inevitable. Over the last 6 years, cloud project revenues grew 20x while traditional hardware database sales dropped 10x.
   - **Tough Job Market:** Organizations have exceptionally high expectations even for interns/freshers. For instance, candidates failed bank intern interviews for not knowing Kubernetes (K8s), and others were rejected by US product companies for being "too green" despite holding certificates and having strong English skills.
   - **Internal Referral Network:** Approximately 90%–100% of jobs at major tech firms like AWS are secured through internal referrals and community networks (like FCAJ) rather than public job boards. The FCAJ community bridges this gap.
   - **AI-based CV Screening:** Resumes are scanned automatically by AI tools, evaluating certificates, public and documented side projects, and the quality of personal blogs to assess system design thinking.
   - **AWS Infrastructure Investment:** AWS is investing heavily in local talent and digital infrastructure in Vietnam, including a Local Zone in Hanoi, CDNs in HCMC and Hanoi, and planning for a large Region.

2. **Data Engineering in Practice** - Speaker: **Vĩnh Bành (Vinh Banh)** - Data Engineer
   
   - **Core Academic Foundations:** Students must master 5 foundational subjects: Databases, Programming Languages, Data Structures and Algorithms, Distributed Databases, and APIs.
   - **School vs. Real-world Differences:**
     - *Data:* School projects use clean and small datasets; real-world data is messy, highly fragmented across multiple sources, and changes constantly.
     - *Requirements & Timeline:* Real-world requirements shift continuously based on business goals, and deadlines are extremely tight (unlike flexible school timelines).
     - *Consequences:* Failing at school costs points or requires retaking a course; failing in the industry impacts production environments and directly disrupts business revenue.
   - **The Iceberg Analogy:** 90% of data challenges lie below the surface: failure handling, data quality, security of sensitive data, and PII management.
   - **AI in the Workflow:** Utilizing AI tools (like Kiro for AWS) alongside Model Context Protocol (MCP) to connect official AWS documentation to scan code and evaluate system architecture against the AWS Well-Architected Framework.
   - **5-Year Career Journey:** Insights drawn from working in startups (taking ownership at Siêu Việt Group), large enterprises (understanding business requirements at Heineken), Fintech (scalability thinking at ZaloPay), fast-paced environments (Xanh SM), and system design consulting (Renova Cloud).

3. **Career Opportunities & Overcoming Workplace Fears** - Speaker: **Như Trần** - Account Manager at AWS Vietnam
   
   - **Unique Personal Identity:** Do not try to become a copy of icons like Steve Jobs or Elon Musk. Focus on understanding and developing your own unique strengths.
   - **Facing Workplace Fears:**
     - *Fear of Mistakes:* It is actually a fear of consequences and judgment from family or society. Learn to take responsibility and actively correct mistakes.
     - *Fear of Public Speaking:* The only way to overcome this is treating it as an "internship"—practicing frequently under high-pressure environments.
   - **Communication & Miscommunication:**
     - Miscommunication occurs when wavelengths do not align. Look deeper to understand the intent behind a manager's or customer's question.
     - When missing a KPI, instead of avoiding your manager, present a clear recovery plan to bridge the gap.
   - **Building Visibility through Small Talk:** Have the courage to start short conversations (greeting managers or teachers in elevators/offices) to build rapport.
   - **Blue Ocean Strategy:** Search for opportunities in less crowded market niches. She shared her personal journey of getting rejected 10 times at Amazon and succeeding on the 11th try by showing coachability and building a trusted network.

4. **Skills, Mindset, and AI for Freshers** - Speaker: **Khang Nguyễn** - Solution Architect at Cloud Kinetics
   
   - **Proper AI Tool Application:**
     - Never outsource your thinking to AI. You can use AI to write code or drafts faster, but you cannot ask AI to understand concepts on your behalf.
     - AI acts as an amplifier: if your foundation is weak, it amplifies your errors; if your foundation is strong, it amplifies your productivity dramatically.
   - **Crucial Core Classes:** Subjects like Computer Networks (Networking), Security, and Cloud Computing are paramount. Study deeply to build capability, not just to pass exams.
   - **School vs. Work Mindset:** School is where you pay to be allowed to make mistakes and learn. Work is where you are paid not to make mistakes.
   - **Self-Ownership & Integrity:**
     - *Stay Hungry:* Go beyond exam parameters or curriculum guidelines by learning advanced modules.
     - *Integrity:* Optimize your projects as best as you can even if it does not increase your school grade.
   - **Three Circles of Career:** Find a balance between Passion, Responsibility, and Benefits.
   - **Long-term Benefits:** Starting salary is not everything; focus on accumulating: Experience, Network, Knowledge, and Personal Growth.
   - **5 Evaluation Criteria for Talent:** Attitude (ranked 1st), Technical Competency, Experience, Exposure, and Talent.

## 3. Key Takeaways & Personal Contribution

This study tour and deep-dive webinar provided me with invaluable lessons for my future career path:
- **Smart AI Integration:** I recognize that AI is only an amplifier. I will practice critical questioning and research the technical fundamentals myself instead of relying blindly on AI-generated code.
- **Mastering Technical Foundations:** I will dedicate time to thoroughly understanding networking, databases, and security classes, as they serve as the foundation for learning cloud services in practice.
- **Proactive Visibility:** I will actively network and participate in community events like FCAJ, practicing small talk to overcome the fear of judgment and expand my internal referral opportunities.
- **Resilience & Growth Mindset:** I will embrace mistakes as opportunities to learn, maintain consistency in applying for roles, and upgrade my skills continuously instead of getting discouraged by market shifts.
